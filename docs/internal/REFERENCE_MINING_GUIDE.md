# Reference Mining Guide

Status: internal research guide
Last updated: 2026-05-06

Use competitor sites, landing pages, templates, and local service business websites as references only to understand structure and conversion mechanics.

Do not copy branding, wording, design, promises, reviews, layout pixel-for-pixel, or visual identity.

## Purpose

Reference mining should help Auralis improve:

- lead capture
- outreach
- closing
- delivery
- proof
- retention

If a reference does not improve one of those outcomes, ignore it.

## What To Mine

Look for useful business mechanics:

- offer layout
- CTA placement
- form structure
- pricing presentation
- FAQ objections
- trust section ideas
- mobile flow
- landing-page simplicity

## Page Structure Patterns

Study how strong pages order sections:

```text
headline
subheadline
CTA
pricing or offer
trust proof
process
FAQ
form
```

Use this to inform focused Auralis pages later, such as:

```text
/website-design-offer
/local-business-websites
```

## CTA Ideas To Rewrite

Collect button ideas, then rewrite them in the Auralis voice.

Useful reference patterns:

- Get a Free Quote
- Start Your Website
- Book a Free Consultation
- Request a Website Review
- Call Now

Better Auralis versions:

- Get a Free Website Review
- Request a Website Quote
- Start a Website Project
- Ask About Managed Care

## Form Fields To Study

Reference forms should help keep the Auralis form short and useful.

A good Auralis inquiry form should usually ask:

- Name
- Business name
- Email
- Phone
- Existing website, if any
- What do you need?
- Timeline
- Budget range

Do not make the first form too long.

## FAQ Objections To Mine

Mine the objections, not the answers.

Useful FAQ topics:

- How much does a website cost?
- How long does it take?
- Do I need photos?
- Can you update my existing website?
- Do I own the site?
- Do you handle hosting?
- Do you offer monthly support?
- Will I rank on Google?

Auralis answers must stay honest, scoped, and aligned with current business reality.

## Trust Mechanics To Study

Look for trust-building mechanics:

- reviews
- portfolio
- client logos
- process
- guarantee
- local business focus
- before/after examples

Use only what Auralis can honestly support right now:

- demo library
- clear process
- transparent starting prices
- no fake testimonials
- Michigan/local focus
- client proof added only after real delivery

## Pricing Presentation

Study whether references show:

- starting price
- packages
- monthly plan
- discount
- free consultation

Current Auralis pricing truth:

- Website Refresh: $250, Auralis-built sites only
- Basic Website: $500+
- Standard Website: $1,000+
- Managed Hosting and Care: $250/month
- Third-party audit/rebuild/custom quote

Do not undercut Auralis just because a competitor looks cheaper.

## Good Reference Categories

### Local Web Design Competitors

Good for:

- pricing
- CTA
- packages
- local SEO wording
- form flow

### High-Converting Landing Pages

Good for:

- section order
- headline clarity
- CTA repetition
- mobile funnel

### Local Service Business Sites

Good for understanding what client sites need:

- barbershops
- restaurants
- lawn care companies
- contractors
- cleaning companies
- mobile bartending businesses

### Webflow / Framer Templates

Good for:

- visual hierarchy
- section ideas
- modern layout patterns
- mobile spacing

Do not copy templates directly. Use them as layout inspiration only.

## What Not To Mine

Do not copy:

- exact copy
- pixel-for-pixel layout
- brand colors or identity
- fake urgency
- fake reviews
- guaranteed rankings
- unlimited updates
- unlimited revisions
- "we do everything" agency language

Those create bad expectations and weaken Auralis's positioning.

## Rule

Mine the business mechanics, not the surface style.

Reference research should make Auralis clearer, easier to buy from, and easier to deliver through. It should not become endless browsing.
