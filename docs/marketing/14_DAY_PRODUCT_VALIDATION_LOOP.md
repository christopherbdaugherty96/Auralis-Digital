# 14-DAY PRODUCT VALIDATION LOOP

Date: 2026-05-19
Status: Active operating model proposal / documentation only

## Purpose

Define the repeatable Auralis commerce loop for launching, marketing, measuring, and either extending or retiring small product drops.

This workflow is designed for:
- Shopify storefront execution
- Printify product fulfillment
- GitHub-based operating memory
- future Nova governed intelligence support

This is NOT autonomous store operation.
This is NOT an instruction to publish products without review.
This is a manual-approved operating cycle.

---

# Core Model

Every 14 days:
1. Create or select 5 products.
2. Build one focused campaign around those products.
3. Market only those products for 14 days.
4. Review analytics and content signals.
5. Keep winners active for an additional month.
6. Archive, redesign, or pause weak products.
7. Repeat with the next focused drop.

This prevents random POD bloat and forces disciplined validation.

---

# Product Volume Rule

Default batch size:

## 5 products every 2 weeks

Do not scale beyond this until:
- the workflow is repeatable
- analytics are being tracked
- content cadence is consistent
- clear product/category winners emerge

The goal is not maximum uploads.
The goal is maximum learning per product.

Each 14-day campaign should have:
- 1 primary category
- 5 products
- 1 campaign theme
- 1 mockup/media direction
- 1 content schedule
- 1 analytics review
- 1 decision outcome

# Daily Content Routine During Campaign

Minimum daily routine:
- review active products
- generate or select one piece of campaign content
- draft caption/hook
- prepare mockup or visual
- log what was posted or prepared
- record early signal if available

Content can include:
- mockup image
- product carousel
- short-form video idea
- Pinterest pin
- Instagram reel concept
- TikTok hook
- product story
- local-service angle
- dad/operator humor

---

# Media And Mockup Requirements

Each 5-product drop should have at least:
- 1 primary campaign banner
- 1 mockup per product
- 1 lifestyle-style mockup per product if possible
- 3 short-form video concepts
- 5 caption drafts
- 5 Pinterest pin concepts

Mockups should match the product category.

Operator categories should use:
- garage/workshop scenes
- truck/trailer contexts
- lawn stripes
- tools/equipment
- early morning work lighting
- rugged simple backgrounds

Artistic categories should use:
- premium product framing
- symbolic visuals
- atmospheric lighting
- limited-drop presentation

# Analytics Review Timing

## Day 7 Midpoint Review

Check:
- traffic
- product views
- content engagement
- saves
- comments
- link clicks
- early add-to-cart behavior

Do not make final decisions at Day 7 unless a product clearly fails or clearly takes off.

## Day 14 Final Review

Check:
- product views
- add-to-cart events
- sales
- conversion behavior
- strongest content
- strongest product
- strongest category signal
- weakest product
- audience comments/questions

Day 14 produces the decision.

---

# Decision Logic

## Keep For One More Month

Keep products/categories that show:
- sales
- strong traffic
- high product views
- saves/comments
- repeated interest
- strong content response
- clear identity fit

These become monthly winners.

## Redesign And Retest

Use this when:
- concept is good but visual execution is weak
- product gets attention but not clicks
- slogan is strong but product type feels wrong
- category has promise but the design needs work

## Pause Or Archive

Use this when:
- no traffic
- no engagement
- no product-page interest
- weak brand fit
- customer confusion
- poor visual quality

Do not let weak products permanently bloat the store.

---

# Winner Retention Rule

If a product performs well during the 14-day campaign:

## Keep it active and promoted for one additional month.

During that month:
- keep it in the relevant collection
- use it as a comparison benchmark
- test additional content angles
- consider related product variants
- consider a follow-up drop in the same category

After one month:
- keep as evergreen if still performing
- move to archive/limited section if interest fades
- use learning for future drops

# GitHub Operating Memory

Every campaign should create or update records in:
- `active/current_14_day_drop.md`
- `trackers/drop_tracker.csv`
- `trackers/product_performance.csv`
- `trackers/content_calendar.csv`
- `trackers/mockup_queue.csv`
- `trackers/campaign_results.csv`

GitHub is the business memory layer.

Shopify is the storefront.
Printify is the fulfillment layer.
Nova is the future governed intelligence layer.

---

# Future Nova Integration

Future Nova should support this loop by:
- reading Shopify analytics
- summarizing active campaign performance
- generating product/content recommendations
- preparing mockup prompts
- preparing captions
- flagging winners and weak products
- drafting GitHub campaign summaries

Nova should not autonomously publish, delete, or mutate products without governed approval.

The correct pattern remains:

## Draft → Review → Approve → Execute
