# RJ Print Pricing Strategy

Last updated: 2026-06-03

This document defines the pricing approach for RJ Print.

## Pricing Goal

RJ Print pricing should protect margin while staying simple enough to quote quickly.

Pricing must account for:

- material cost
- failed print risk
- design/setup time
- print time
- finishing time
- packaging
- shipping materials
- platform fees
- taxes
- customer support time

## Product Pricing Approach

Use product-level starting prices for repeatable items.

Use custom quotes for:

- personalized designs
- size changes
- complex geometry
- multi-part prints
- rush requests
- unusual colors or materials
- uncertain production time

## Margin Guardrail

Do not price only from material cost.

3D printed products also consume machine time, testing time, design time, finishing time, and support time.

## Discount Guardrail

Do not use fake discounts, fake urgency, or fake scarcity.

Discounts should only be used when the margin still works and the reason is real.

## Quote Rule

If a custom request is unclear, quote after reviewing the design and production requirements.

Do not promise a fixed price before confirming feasibility.
